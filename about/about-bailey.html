About Bailey

One horsewoman.
One farm. One stubborn
refusal to outsource it.

Before there was a market, there was just a girl who didn't like being told what she couldn't figure out on her own.

Where it started

I've always done the thing myself first.

I've spent close to a decade as a farrier — under horses, hands on hooves, learning a trade most people pay someone else to know. That's the theme of my whole life, honestly. If something needs doing, I'd rather learn how to do it than write a check to make it someone else's problem. The leap

I quit my sales job to do this full-time.

There was no safety net moment, no viral video, no investor — just a decision that the version of my life spent in a garden instead of a CRM was worth betting on. Life around here

It's not just me out here.

I share this place with my partner, Neil, who farms the few hundred acres around us — between the two of us, pretty much everything in sight is growing something. [Placeholder: a line on how Neil factors into day-to-day.] Then there's Po, a mini silky fainting goat who has never once helped with anything, and Copper, a cockapoo who supervises from a respectful distance.

Where this is headed

This is year one. Not the whole plan.

Right now it's herbs, flowers, flour, and produce. Down the road, I'd like to bring the horses home and grow this into the place I pictured before any of it existed on paper. From me to you

Thanks for actually reading this far.

If you've made it this far, you probably care about where your food and flowers come from as much as I do. Come find me at market sometime — I'm the one covered in dirt, probably mid-sentence about a goat.
