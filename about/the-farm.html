The Farm

How we grow it.
How we pack it.
What we won't do.

Everything at Bailey's Market is grown, dried, milled, or cut on one small property in Blissfield, Michigan — by one person, by hand. Here's exactly how that works.

01 — Growing

Chemical-free, by method — not by marketing.

No synthetic pesticides, herbicides, or fertilizers anywhere on the property. Pest and weed pressure is managed with crop rotation, companion planting, and mulch instead of a sprayer. The soil is built with compost and worm castings, not borrowed from a bag.

02 — Packaging

As close to zero plastic as a real product allows.

Glass and kraft come first, always. Where neither holds up to the job — like keeping greens crisp for a few weeks — we use BPI-certified compostable plant-based packaging instead of conventional plastic. Quality and shelf life come first, but conventional plastic is never the default.

03 — Labeling

What's on the jar is what's in it.

No filler, no "natural flavor" mystery ingredients, no inflated claims. We are not USDA Certified Organic, and you'll never see that word on a label here — only what's actually true.

04 — Capacity

Small on purpose. Honest about it.

This is a one-person operation on roughly an acre and a half. Batches are limited, certain items sell out, and timelines move with the weather, not a production schedule. We'd rather under-promise and let the harvest speak for itself.
